# THE DOCKS - Story & Setting

## THE BACKSTORY

### Why You're Here

They call it **The Docks** — though the shipping container cranes haven't worked in fifteen years. Now they just rust against the sky like skeleton fingers pointing at nowhere.

You grew up in the forgotten corners of a dying industrial city. Your mother worked two jobs at the canneries down by the waterfront. Your father was never in the picture—just a name scrawled on a birth certificate and a photograph you keep folded in your wallet. The city used to hum with factories and prosperity. Now those same factories are hollow husks, their smokestacks cold monuments to a world that moved on without you.

You just got out of state correctional — three years for possession with intent. Not anything serious, just bad luck and a worse lawyer. The kind of charge that ruins your life but doesn't make you a king. You'd do the time, get out, and find the world had moved on without you.

Your apartment's gone. Your girl's gone. Your "crew" dissolved the moment your cell door clicked shut. Old friends suddenly don't return calls. The streets you grew up on are run by people who've never seen you before.

But here's what they don't know: you learned things inside. You met people. You figured out how the real game works. And you've got nothing left to lose.

Six months ago, you took a one-way bus ticket to The Docks with $87 in your pocket, a stolen bicycle, and the phone number of a cousin you hadn't spoken to in five years. That cousin is dead now—overdose in a safehouse somewhere in Sector 7—but the contacts he left behind? They're still here. And they remember anyone who was "family."

You're not special. You're not connected. You're just someone who got tired of being hungry.

### Your Goal

Simple: Make enough money to get out. Or make enough money to stay and actually matter.

The Docks isn't a place you "win." It's a place you survive long enough to build something. Your goal is to become the biggest operator in The Docks—not through violence, but through connections, reputation, and cold hard cash. Every dollar you make is a vote of confidence from the streets. Every product you move is a statement.

Reach **$50,000** and you've "made it." Until then, you're just another face in the crowd, trying not to get clipped.

### What Drives You

- **Survival**: The city doesn't care about you. Every day you wake up is a day you didn't get arrested, didn't get robbed, didn't get killed.
- **Reputation**: In The Docks, respect is currency. The more names you make for yourself, the more doors open.
- **Greed**: Let's be honest. You didn't come here to live modestly. You came here to get rich.
- **Vengeance** (optional, player-driven): Your cousin didn't just die—he was killed. Someone in The Docks made that happen. You can choose to investigate... or you can focus on the money. Both paths are valid.

## THE CITY: THE DOCKS

### Overview

The Docks is a sprawling waterfront district on the east side of an unnamed crumbling city. Once the economic heart of the region—where ships arrived laden with goods from across the world—it's now a shadow of its former self. The port authority went bankrupt twelve years ago. The shipping companies moved their operations to cleaner, cheaper locales. What remained was a labyrinth of abandoned warehouses, decrepit apartment blocks, and streets that remember when things were better.

The city still sends crews to maintain basic infrastructure—pothole filling, streetlight replacement, the occasional sweep—but they're fighting a losing battle. Graffiti tags cover every vertical surface. Broken glass crunches underfoot. The smell of saltwater mixes with diesel fuel, rotting produce, and something chemical you learned not to ask about.

The population is a mix of:
- **Old-timers**: Retired dock workers and their families, too stubborn or too poor to leave
- **Immigrants**: Recent arrivals who end up in the cheapest housing available
- **Ex-cons**: People on probation who can't leave the city limits
- **Hustlers**: Anyone looking to make quick money in the grey and black markets
- **The desperate**: Addicts, the homeless, people running from something

The Docks never sleeps, but it does slow down. Business peaks at night and in the early morning hours. Midday is for sleeping, hiding, or laying low.

### Key Locations

#### Safehouse Areas
- **The Flats** (Central Docks): Cheap apartment buildings, thin walls, lots of foot traffic. Your starting area. Safe enough if you keep your head down.
- **The Saltworks** (North Docks): Abandoned salt processing facility converted into a shanty town. Dangerous but anonymous.
- **Pier 9** (Waterfront): Warehouse district with secret tunnels connecting to the piers. Good for quick escapes.

#### Supplier Meet Spots
- **The Old Mill**: Massive abandoned textile factory, now neutral ground for faction meetings
- **Container City**: Stacked shipping containers forming a maze-like settlement
- **The Roost**: Rooftop garden/meeting spot controlled by information brokers

#### Customer Areas
- **The Strip**: Row of dive bars and pawn shops, prime selling territory
- **The Market**: Open-air black market operating every full moon
- **Underpass**: Homeless encampment and underground trading post

#### Points of Interest
- **The Lighthouse**: Abandoned lighthouse with a clear view of the entire district—great for planning, terrible for privacy
- **Foundry Square**: Central plaza where gang territories meet, neutral ground for big deals
- **The Graveyard**: Old cemetery where people go to mourn... or to make deals far from prying eyes

## GAME TIMELINE

### Day 1-3: The Struggle
You arrive with nothing. You need to learn the basics: where to buy, where to sell, how to avoid the cops. Your first suppliers are small-time—you buy cheap, sell cheap, and pray you don't get caught.

### Day 4-10: Building
You've got a routine now. You know which buyers are safe and which will rob you. You've met at least a few faction representatives. Time to start building relationships and expanding your operations.

### Day 11-20: The Wars
The factions start noticing you. They want you to work for them, or against them. Major players start offering exclusive deals—and demanding loyalty. Choosing sides has consequences.

### Day 21+: The Takeover
If you've played your cards right, you're in a position to challenge the existing order. The final showdown awaits. Win, and you control The Docks. Lose, and you start over—from scratch, or from a grave.

## ATMOSPHERE & TONE

The Docks is:
- **Gritty**: Every surface is dirty. Every deal is risky. Nothing comes easy.
- **Real**: No magic, no superheroes, no sci-fi. Just people trying to survive.
- **Morally Grey**: You're not a good person in this game. You're a criminal. The question isn't "are you evil?" but "how far are you willing to go?"
- **Alive**: The world changes around you. Factions rise and fall. Your reputation matters.

The Docks isn't just a setting—it's a character. It has moods (fog rolling in at night, rain turning streets to rivers, the hum of generators and distant music). It has secrets (tunnels, hidden stashes, bodies in the water). It remembers everything.
