# FACTIONS.md — The Twelve Families

## Gangs of The Docks

---

Twelve factions rule The Docks. Some are old money, passed down through generations. Some are new blood, risen from the ashes of the last war. All of them want what you want: control.

Here's who they are, where they operate, and why they'd kill you — or hire you.

---

### 1. The Don — The Old Family

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Old Town — the Victorian district with its brownstones and hidden wine cellars |
| **Specialty** | Import/Export — legitimate businesses masking serious cargo |
| **Leader** | Don Moretti, 68, hasn't left his estate in years but still runs everything |
| **Allies** | Frost (smuggling routes), The Old Family has no real friends — only useful contacts |
| **Enemies** | Everyone who owes them money, which is almost everyone |

**The Vibe:** Old-world Italian crime. They own restaurants, import companies, a few city councilmen. They don't do street-level product — they're above that. But they control the ports, and without their blessing, nothing comes into The Docks by sea.

**Quest Givers:** Moretti's son, Luca, handles the day-to-day. He's looking for someone to move product without drawing police attention. Do his dirty work, and you get access to their shipping routes.

---

### 2. Viper — The Serpents

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Skid Row — the alleyways and abandoned SROs where the desperate gather |
| **Specialty** | Poison & Designer Drugs — synthetic stuff, laced product, chemical research |
| **Leader** | Viper (real name: Dr. Elena Marsh), a former pharmaceutical researcher turned chemist |
| **Allies** | Blaze (distribution network), Shade (black market supplies) |
| **Enemies** | The Ice (territory disputes in the old industrial zone) |

**The Vibe:** Clinical. Precise. Viper's product is clean — which is rare in The Docks — and her prices reflect that. She's not interested in violence; she's interested in science. But her customers are the most desperate, most unpredictable people in the city.

**Quest Givers:** Viper needs ingredients. Dangerous ingredients. Sometimes she needs people to test new batches. Always she needs protection from competitors who'd love to steal her formulas.

---

### 3. Rook — The Crown

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Old Town — underground gambling halls hidden behind Brownstone storefronts |
| **Specialty** | Gambling — underground casinos, high-stakes poker, sports betting |
| **Leader** | Rook (born Marcus Thornwood), a chess prodigy who turned his mind to probability |
| **Allies** | The Don (money laundering), Byte (fixed games) |
| **Enemies** | The Ice (lost a fortune in a card game once), anyone who cheats |

**The Vibe:** Elegant. Sophisticated. Rook's establishments are the only places in The Docks where you can get a decent drink and not fear getting robbed walking out. That's by design. He wants a clean operation. Professional. But the house always wins, and Rook's very, very good at math.

**Quest Givers:** Rook needs collectors. People who can make examples of debtors without making messes. He also runs a weekly high-stakes tournament — winner takes serious cash, but the buy-in is brutal.

---

### 4. Ghost — The Ravens

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Everywhere and nowhere — they don't hold territory, they operate in all zones |
| **Specialty** | Assassination & Intel — wet work, surveillance, information brokering |
| **Leader** | Ghost (no one knows their real name), communicates only through encrypted channels |
| **Allies** | Nobody — they sell to anyone who can pay |
| **Enemies** | Everyone fears them, nobody trusts them |

**The Vibe:** Shadowy. Ghost's people don't show their faces. They communicate through dead drops, burner phones, messages hidden in plain sight. They know things about everyone — and they're willing to sell those secrets to the highest bidder. They've never been caught. They've never been identified. Some people aren't sure they exist.

**Quest Givers:** Ghost doesn't do repeat customers — it's too risky. But they occasionally take contracts, and they'll pay premium for results. They also sell information: want to know who in your organization is talking to rivals? Ghost can find out.

---

### 5. Iron — The Iron Hands

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Ironworks — the old shipyard and auto repair shops along the waterfront |
| **Specialty** | Chop Shops — stolen vehicles, stripped parts, ghost titles |
| **Leader** | Iron (real name: Big Tony Ferro), a mechanic who built an empire from scrap |
| **Allies** | Razor (weapons deals), Fang (scrap metal and stolen goods) |
| **Enemies** | The police (obviously), Frost (competition for waterfront space) |

**The Vibe:** Grease and blood. Iron's operation is old-school: steal cars, strip them, sell the parts. But they've moved up in the world — now they do customized vehicles, armored cars, anything with an engine. Tony's a craftsman. He takes pride in his work.

**Quest Givers:** Iron needs drivers. People who can "acquire" vehicles without getting caught. He also has connections to the junkyards — places where bodies (and evidence) disappear.

---

### 6. Fang — The Jackals

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | The Flats — low-income housing blocks, Salvage Yard, the Maw's surface streets |
| **Specialty** | Scavenging — anything that can be sold, salvaged, or repurposed |
| **Leader** | Fang (real name: Carla "Fang" Okonkwo), a former sanitation worker who knows every dumpster and dump in The Docks |
| **Allies** | Iron (parts), Shade (fencing stolen goods), Storm (nomads bring scrap) |
| **Enemies** | Blaze (territory disputes), the city (illegal dumping enforcement) |

**The Vibe:** Scrappy. Resourceful. Fang's people can find value in anything — a discarded phone, a half-empty chemical drum, a dead body's watch. They're not criminals in the traditional sense; they're survivors. But they know where bodies are buried. Literally.

**Quest Givers:** Fang needs runners. People who can move product through the Flats without attracting attention. She also has a network of "finders" — people who report when something valuable shows up in the wrong place.

---

### 7. Storm — The Drift

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | The Harbor — houseboats, docked ships, the camps on the abandoned piers |
| **Specialty** | Nomad Lifestyle — they move constantly, smuggling by boat, running when things heat up |
| **Leader** | Storm (real name: Captain Sara Chen), former merchant marine, lost her license and found a different way to sail |
| **Allies** | Frost (water routes), The Don (offshore transfers) |
| **Enemies** | The Ice (competition for smuggling routes), the Coast Guard |

**The Vibe:** Free. Storm's people live on the water — literally. They don't hold territory because they don't need to. They can appear anywhere along the coastline, move product by boat, and vanish before anyone knows they were there. They don't fight for streets. They fight for routes.

**Quest Givers:** Storm needs people who can handle boats — or at least handle themselves on the water. She also needs safe harbors, places to lay low when the law gets too close.

---

### 8. Shade — The Shadows

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | The Maw — underground tunnels, abandoned subway stations, the catacombs beneath Old Town |
| **Specialty** | Black Market — anything illegal, anything forbidden, anything that can't be sold above ground |
| **Leader** | Shade (real name: Yusuf Hassan), a former black-market broker who knows everyone who owes everyone |
| **Allies** | Everyone who needs something they can't get legally |
| **Enemies** | The police (underground operations), Rook (competing for underground economy) |

**The Vibe:** Underground. Literally. Shade runs the tunnels beneath The Docks — passages that date back to the city's founding. There's a whole economy down there: black market goods, unlicensed medical treatment, fighting rings, everything prohibited above ground. Shade controls access. Pay the toll, and you can move through their territory.

**Quest Givers:** Shade needs runners to move product through the tunnels. They also fence stolen goods — everything from hot electronics to stolen art. If you need something unusual, Shade can find it.

---

### 9. Blaze — The Embers

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Skid Row and parts of The Flats — the poorest, most desperate neighborhoods |
| **Specialty** | Meth & Coke — high-volume production, aggressive distribution |
| **Leader** | Blaze (real name: Derek "Blaze" Williams), a former cook who learned chemistry in prison |
| **Allies** | Viper (research), Fang (distribution through the Flats), Razor (protection) |
| **Enemies** | The Ice (territory), the police, anyone who tries to move on their blocks |

**The Vibe:** Violent. Aggressive. Blaze runs the hardest product in The Docks — meth that's cheap and coke that's strong. Their people are everywhere, selling on corners, pushing product to anyone with cash. They don't negotiate. They dominate. If you try to sell on their turf, they make examples.

**Quest Givers:** Blaze needs distributors. They also need protection when things go wrong — and things go wrong often. They're not choosy about who they hire, but they're very choosy about loyalty.

---

### 10. Frost — The Ice

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | The Harbor and portions of the Industrial Zone — cold storage facilities, shipping containers |
| **Specialty** | Smuggling — people, product, anything that fits in a container |
| **Leader** | Frost (real name: Viktor "Ice" Petrov), a former Russian smuggler who came to America thirty years ago and never left |
| **Allies** | Storm (water routes), The Don (port access), Razor (weapon smuggling) |
| **Enemies** | Blaze (territory), Viper (competition for chemical smuggling), the feds |

**The Vibe:** Cold. Professional. Frost's operation is the most organized in The Docks — they work like a corporation, with schedules, routes, and protocols. They move product in shipping containers, hide it in cold storage, and have people at every checkpoint. He's the closest thing The Docks has to a legitimate businessman.

**Quest Givers:** Frost needs people who can handle logistics. Loading docks, manifestos, the boring paperwork of crime. But he also needs muscle — sometimes shipments get intercepted, and he needs people who can "recover" product.

---

### 11. Razor — The Blades

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | The Industrial Zone — factories, warehouses, the decommissioned power plant |
| **Specialty** | Weapons — trafficking, manufacturing, modification |
| **Leader** | Razor (real name: Sgt. Maria Santos), a former arms dealer who got out of the military with a shopping list and a grudge |
| **Allies** | Iron (custom weapon modifications), Frost (shipping), Blaze (protection) |
| **Enemies** | The police, the feds, anyone who tries to compete in weapons |

**The Vibe:** Military. Efficient. Razor doesn't mess around. She supplies half the gangs in The Docks with hardware — pistols, rifles, the occasional heavy weapon. Her stuff is reliable, well-maintained, and expensive. She also modifies weapons, turning everyday firearms into something special.

**Quest Givers:** Razor needs runners to move weapons — which is the most dangerous product to handle. She also needs "clean" people who can buy components without raising flags. Get on her good side, and you've got a weapons supplier for life.

---

### 12. Byte — The Network

| | |
|---|---|
| **Territory** | Everywhere with WiFi — they operate from hidden server rooms and van-based mobile units |
| **Specialty** | Hacking — data theft, corporate espionage, digital forensics, cryptocurrency |
| **Leader** | Byte (real name: They go by "Zero"), a former security researcher who discovered that crime pays better than disclosure |
| **Allies** | Rook (fixed games), The Don (corporate espionage), Shade (digital security for black market) |
| **Enemies** | The feds (cybercrime division), anyone who doesn't understand the digital world |

**The Vibe:** Digital. Anonymous. Byte's people don't carry weapons — they carry laptops. They hack bank accounts, steal corporate secrets, launder money through cryptocurrency, and provide "digital services" to anyone who can pay. They're the newest faction in The Docks, and they're growing fast.

**Quest Givers:** Byte needs people who can physically access buildings — they can hack anything, but sometimes they need someone to plug in a device or steal a server. They also need "clean" identities, people without digital footprints who can do things in the real world.

## Named Quest Givers

These are the specific NPCs who will give you missions, errands, and opportunities throughout The Docks.

### Luca Moretti — The Don's Son
- **Affiliation:** The Old Family
- **Role:** Operations manager
- **Description:** The young heir to the Moretti empire. Sharp but impatient, Luca wants to prove himself to his father by any means necessary. He handles the family's legitimate businesses as cover for moving product — restaurants, import companies, shipping manifests.
- **Quest Types:** Safe delivery runs, establishing new drop points, protecting shipments

### Denny "Flick" Vance — The Crown's Collector
- **Affiliation:** The Crown
- **Role:** Debt collector and enforcement
- **Description:** A former poker prodigy who lost everything and now works for Rook. Flick has a mechanical eye and a short temper. He collects debts from those who owe the Crown — and makes examples of those who don't pay.
- **Quest Types:** Debt collection, intimidating debtors, investigating card cheaters

### Junebug — Viper's Lab Assistant
- **Affiliation:** The Serpents
- **Role:** Procurement and field testing
- **Description:** A gaunt young person who speaks too fast and sweats too much. Junebug has been with Viper long enough to know which experiments are safe and which will kill you. They handle sourcing rare chemicals from sketchy suppliers.
- **Quest Types:** Sourcing chemicals, test deliveries, retrieving stolen formulas

*Know your enemies. Know your allies. Know who'll stab you in the back at the first opportunity.*

*That's how you survive in The Docks.*
